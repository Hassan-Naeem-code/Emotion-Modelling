\title{Knowing When It Doesn't Know:\\
       Calibrated, Uncertainty-Aware Affect Estimation under Distribution Shift}
\author{Muhammad Hassan Naeem}

\begin{abstract}
We estimate continuous valence and arousal (VA) from faces while \emph{quantifying
how much to trust each estimate}. Our primary model is a deep evidential
regressor that yields aleatoric and epistemic uncertainty in a single forward
pass; we calibrate prediction intervals with split conformal prediction for
finite-sample coverage guarantees, and abstain when uncertainty exceeds the
calibrated bound. We evaluate calibration (regression ECE), selective prediction
(risk--coverage, AURC), and behaviour under distribution shift. Under a synthetic
out-of-distribution benchmark that exercises the full pipeline, uncertainty-aware
models retain calibration and coverage where a deterministic baseline does not;
on real data we report an in-distribution AffectNet evidential model and release
the complete AffectNet$\rightarrow$AFEW-VA harness for the cross-dataset shift
evaluation. An on-device browser demo exposes the same calibrated uncertainty and
abstention to end users with no data leaving the machine.
\end{abstract}
