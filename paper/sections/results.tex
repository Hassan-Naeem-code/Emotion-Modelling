\paragraph{Calibration and abstention.} The deterministic baseline provides no
usable uncertainty (degenerate ECE, near-chance OOD-AUROC), whereas the
evidential and conformal variants are well calibrated and let selective
prediction trade coverage for risk. Figures~\ref{fig:rc} and \ref{fig:rel} show
the risk--coverage curve and reliability diagram for the evidential model.

\paragraph{Under shift.} Moving from in-distribution to the shifted test set,
the uncertainty-aware models raise their predictive uncertainty and retain
conformal coverage closer to target than the baseline, evidencing that the
system flags its own degradation rather than failing silently.
