\section{Results}

\paragraph{Status of reported numbers.} We distinguish two regimes. The
cross-method comparison in Table~\ref{tab:comparison} is produced on the
synthetic VA generator, which validates the full train$\rightarrow$uncertainty
$\rightarrow$conformal$\rightarrow$metrics machinery end to end; these numbers
demonstrate that each method behaves as designed, not state-of-the-art accuracy.
Separately, we train the primary evidential head on real
AffectNet~\cite{mollahosseini2017affectnet} and report its in-distribution
calibration and selective-prediction behaviour. The cross-dataset shift
evaluation (AffectNet$\rightarrow$AFEW-VA) uses the same harness and is reported
once AFEW-VA access is in place; the pipeline and configuration for it are
released.

Table~\ref{tab:comparison} compares methods across accuracy, calibration,
selective prediction, shift detection, and conformal coverage. The table and all
figures are generated by \texttt{eval/aggregate.py} and \texttt{eval/run\_eval.py};
re-running \texttt{make repro} overwrites them in place.

\paragraph{Calibration and abstention.} The deterministic baseline provides no
usable uncertainty (degenerate ECE, near-chance OOD-AUROC), whereas the
evidential and conformal variants are well calibrated and let selective
prediction trade coverage for risk. Figures~\ref{fig:rc} and \ref{fig:rel} show
the risk--coverage curve and reliability diagram for the evidential model.

\paragraph{Under shift.} On the synthetic out-of-distribution split, moving from
in-distribution to shifted inputs, the uncertainty-aware models raise their
predictive uncertainty and retain conformal coverage closer to target than the
baseline, evidencing that the system flags its own degradation rather than
failing silently. For real data, we evaluate a corruption-based shift of the
AffectNet test set (blur/noise/brightness/contrast/JPEG, ImageNet-C style) with
the released harness; because it perturbs the same images and labels, it isolates
the effect of shift on calibration without confounding from a different dataset.
The optional cross-dataset AffectNet$\rightarrow$AFEW-VA shift runs identically
where that registration-gated data is available.
