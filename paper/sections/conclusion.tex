\section{Conclusion}

We presented a calibrated, uncertainty-aware approach to continuous valence and
arousal estimation that knows when it does not know. Deep evidential regression
supplies aleatoric and epistemic uncertainty in one pass, split conformal
prediction turns that uncertainty into intervals with coverage guarantees and a
principled abstention threshold, and our evaluation centres on calibration and
distribution shift rather than accuracy alone. The same calibrated bound powers
an on-device demonstrator that abstains under poor signal and never transmits
imagery. Limitations include reliance on the synthetic generator for CI-time
validation and a single-window rPPG front-end; extending the gated physiological
modality to full video and broadening the shift evaluation are natural next
steps.
